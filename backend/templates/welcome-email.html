Your Free Nepal Trip Planning Checklist

Before You Go

☐  Apply for Nepal visa online (Tourist On Arrival or e-Visa)
☐  Get travel insurance covering high-altitude trekking
☐  Book Trekkers' Information Management System (TIMS) card
☐  Arrange Annapurna / Langtang / Everest area permits as needed
☐  Check vaccination requirements (Hepatitis A&B, Typhoid, Rabies)
☐  Book flights to Kathmandu (TIA) and internal flights if needed
☐  Register your trip with your country's embassy

Packing Essentials

☐  Moisture-wicking base layers (wool or synthetic)
☐  Insulating mid-layer (fleece or down jacket)
☐  Waterproof outer shell / rain poncho
☐  Sturdy trekking boots (broken in before the trip)
☐  Trekking poles for steep descents
☐  Sleeping bag rated to -10°C for high-altitude lodges
☐  First-aid kit (blister pads, Diamox, ibuprofen, rehydration salts)
☐  Headlamp with spare batteries
☐  Sunscreen SPF 50+, lip balm, UV sunglasses

Money & Connectivity

☐  Withdraw Nepalese Rupees (NPR) at Kathmandu airport ATM
☐  Carry USD cash as backup — card payments accepted in restaurants and hotels in major cities only
☐  Get a Ncell or NTC SIM card at the airport
☐  Download offline maps (Maps.me or Google Maps offline area)
☐  Budget NPR 2,000–4,000/day for teahouse trekking
☐  Note that ATMs are rare above 3,000 m — carry enough cash

Health & Safety

☐  Learn altitude sickness symptoms (AMS, HACE, HAPE)
☐  Follow "climb high, sleep low" acclimatisation rule
☐  Drink 3–4 litres of water daily at altitude
☐  Use water purification tablets or filter bottle
☐  Save emergency contact: CIWEC Clinic +977 1 4435232
☐  Know the nearest helicopter rescue evacuation points on your route
☐  Never trek alone in remote areas — hire a licensed guide

Cultural Etiquette

☐  Remove shoes before entering temples and homes
☐  Dress modestly (cover shoulders and knees at religious sites)
☐  Walk clockwise around stupas and mani walls
☐  Ask permission before photographing people or ceremonies
☐  Use right hand or both hands when giving / receiving items

Top Things to Do

☐  Kathmandu Valley — Pashupatinath, Boudhanath, Swayambhunath
☐  Annapurna Circuit or Annapurna Base Camp trek
☐  Everest Base Camp trek via Lukla
☐  Chitwan National Park — jungle safari and rhino spotting
☐  Pokhara — Phewa Lake, World Peace Pagoda, paragliding
☐  Lumbini — birthplace of Buddha, UNESCO World Heritage Site
☐  Sunrise at Nagarkot or Sarangkot viewpoints

Best Time to Visit

☐  Oct–Nov (Peak): Clear skies, ideal for trekking and mountain views
☐  Mar–May (Spring): Rhododendrons bloom, great trekking, warmer
☐  Dec–Feb (Winter): Cold at altitude, quiet trails, cheaper rates
☐  Jun–Sep (Monsoon): Lush green landscapes, leeches on trails, avoid high passes
☐  Book flights and lodges 3–6 months ahead for Oct–Nov season
